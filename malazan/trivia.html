Adjunct
The Adjunct serves as the Malazan Throne's supreme field commander and voice, empowered to lead Imperial legions, enforce edicts, and depose generals, while their otataral weapon nullifies sorcery around them.
Ahkrast Korvalain
Forkrul Assail Hold. It granted its wielders' voices great magical powers over the psyche. The hold itself was thought to create the ability for thought and consciousness in a world where thought never existed before.
Aral Gamelon
The Path of Demons.
Ascendant
Ascendants were individuals who had transcended death. In the time of the First Empire they had been called First Heroes. Ascendants could become gods if they gained sufficient following among mortals, but they were not gods by default. Those without worshippers were considered unchained, or Unaligned in the language of the Deck of Dragons. Gods who lost their worshippers remained ascendant, but were effectively emasculated unless worship was somehow renewed.
Bonecaster
A Bonecaster is a shaman of the Imass people, and later the T'lan Imass. They are practitioners of magic, with T'lan Imass Bonecasters specifically utilizing the Warren of Tellann. Historically, they played a crucial role in the ritual that transformed the Imass into the undead T'lan Imass. Their abilities include 'spiritwalking' to perceive visions of the present and future, and sensing the earth to locate others or track prey. They are sometimes identified by their characteristic lack of weapons.
Chance
A sword dedicated to Oponn, the Twin Jesters of Chance. This weapon embodies the chaotic nature of luck and probability.
Claw
The Claw is the elite intelligence and assassination agency of the Malazan Empire, answering directly to the Empress. They are a highly secretive and feared organization, known for their ruthless efficiency and unwavering loyalty. Claws are trained in a variety of skills, including espionage, infiltration, and assassination. They operate both within the Empire, to root out rebellion and corruption, and externally, to advance the Empire's interests. Each Claw undergoes intense physical and psychological conditioning, and their hands are surgically altered to resemble talons, which they use as deadly weapons.
D'ivers
A higher order of shape-shifting that allows an individual to take multiple forms simultaneously, splitting into many separate beings. This is more complex and dangerous than Soletaken transformation.
Deadhouse Warren
A specific section or gateway within the Imperial Warren.
Deck of Dragons
The Deck of Dragons, sometimes called the Fatid, was the dominant oracular system across the world (except Lether, which used the Tiles); it could one day be succeeded by the Coins. The Deck comprised cards depicting members of the pantheon, adding new ones as needed, mostly aligned to Houses with a few unaligned cards that could even animate during a reading.

Dragnipur
A sword wielded by Anomander Rake, forged by the Elder God Draconus. This legendary weapon traps the souls of those it slays within it, chaining them to pull a wagon bearing the Gate of Darkness for eternity.
Drift Avalii
Drift Avalii is a mysterious and ancient island, believed by some to be a former flying fortress. It is a significant location in the Malazan world, holding many secrets, including the presence of a mysterious 'sleeper' within it. The island is a place of power and is associated with the Throne of Shadow.

Finnest
An object used as a repository of power by a Jaghut. These artifacts contain immense magical energy and can be extremely dangerous if disturbed or misused.
Great Raven
The Great Ravens were massive ravens, larger than the norm, who were "born like maggots from the rotting corpse-flesh of the Chained God when he was pulled down into the world". As a result, they carried within them a fragment of his power. They were largely impervious to most forms of magic, with chaos being a notable exception. For them, magic was 'ambrosia', and if attacked with sorcery they would only grow stronger, more immune, and their life-spans - already centuries-long - would be even more extended.
High Fist
The title of a commander of armies in a Malazan Campaign. The High Fist was the highest ranking officer in a theatre of operations. Its naval equivalent rank was an Admiral. A High Fist could only be overruled by the Emperor or Empress him/herself or, by extension, his or her Adjunct.
High Mage
The title of High Mage was used for both military and civilian mages. In the military, it typically referred to the commander of a mage cadre in the Malazan Army. Among civilians, it was used for mages with particularly powerful skills. Some individuals were considered "Natural High Mages" for their talent, even if not officially recognized. The highest rank was the Imperial High Mage, the top-ranking mage in the entire Malazan Army.

Moon's Spawn
Also known as the Tiste Andii's Fang of Darkness, was a floating fortress or skykeep containing a city commanded by the Tiste Andii. According to Anomander Rake, the city was home to children, priests, and several scholarly warlocks. The fortress sheltered a vast flock of Great Ravens that nested in the crags and caverns of its basalt surface, and within its inner chambers lay the lair of Silanah, the Eleint.

It appeared as a vertically-shaped oblong with no visible signs of construction on its exterior, apart from a small ledge with a recessed doorway leading inside. Tattersail described it as an enormous floating mountain, ragged as a blackened tooth and battered by time. While stationed above Pale, Moon's Spawn revolved steadily until the beginning of the final battle, when the hidden portal appeared near the top.

Its floating nature rendered it immune to siege, and even the undead Logros T'lan Imass were unwilling or unable to assault its magical defenses.

Otataral
A rare, reddish ore negating magic traditionally wielded by Imperial Adjuncts. It also prevented mages from accessing or opening their Warrens and prolonged exposure to refined ore could drive them insane. It is not clear if this was due to the Otataral itself or to the lack of access to their Warren. Even after long exposure, mages might be able to regain their powers as long as they were removed from the Otataral source, especially if the Otataral was of low quality.

Warren
A realm from which mages, priests, shamans, and the like, who might use magic, drew their power. Structurally these Warrens were divided into two classes: Elder Warrens, which were associated with a particular race; and the Paths, which were those Warrens most commonly accessed by humans and which were derived from K'rul's blood. However, not all realms described as Warrens fit that description; The Abyss was named a Warren but seemed to be used only as a means of travel rather than a source of power. It is also possible that there was a difference on how some Warrens like the Abyss were created.

"The Warrens of Magic dwelt in the beyond. Find the gate and nudge it open a crack. What leaks out is yours to shape. Open yourself to the Warren that comes to you - that finds you. Draw forth its power - as much as your body and soul are capable of containing - but remember, when the body fails, the gate closes." (Tattersail)
Warrens of Chaos
The miasmic paths between the Warrens, turbulent and dangerous transitional spaces where the boundaries between magical realms blur and dissolve into raw chaos.
