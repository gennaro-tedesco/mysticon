Barghast
The Barghast are a nomadic warrior society of non-human origin, physically imposing with features reminiscent of their Imass ancestors. Their culture is organized into rival clans, each with a rich history recorded in intricate tattoos. Guided by shamans who commune with ancestral spirits, they are known for their martial prowess and sexually free customs. A core tenet of their existence is a holy war promised by their ancestor-gods. "Those boys will fight anyone, anywhere."
Brethen
Tribal people of northern Genabackis.
Bright Knot
Northern tribal people.
Daru
The Daru are the inhabitants of Darujhistan, the 'City of Blue Fire' on Genabackis. Renowned for their ingenuity, they have harnessed natural gas for lighting and industry. Their city is an oligarchy governed by a council of nobles, and the Daru people are known for their resilience and independent spirit, thriving in a metropolis of political intrigue and ancient secrets.
Eleint
The Eleint are an ancient and powerful race of pure dragons in the Malazan world, originating from the Elder Warren of Starvald Demelain. They are considered fragments of the goddess T'iam, bound by shared histories and bloodlines, but driven by rage due to their splintered natures.
Fildasz
Northern tribal people.
Forkrul Assail
The Forkrul Assail are an extinct mythical people, one of the Four Founding Races of the Malazan world. Known for their pursuit of absolute justice and order, they wielded immense power through their voices and the Hold of Ahkrast Korvalain.
Gadrobi
Indigenous people of central Genabackis.
Gandaru
The Gandaru are a tribal people from the Lamatath Plain, closely related to the Kindaru. They are a near-extinct people, characterized by their sloped brows, tawny-hued eyes, and a wary, animal-like demeanor. Their features are a unique blend of human and animal, suggesting an alternate version of humanity. With dark skin and broad, expressive mouths, the Gandaru are a fleeting presence in the world.
Ganrel
Tribal people of northern Genabackis.
Genabarii
The Genabarii are a cultural group and language found in northwest Genabackis. The city of Genabaris serves as a major settlement for this people.
Jaghut
The Jaghut are an extinct mythical people, one of the Four Founding Races of the Malazan world. Solitary by nature and masters of ice magic through Omtose Phellack, they were hunted nearly to extinction by the T'lan Imass in an eternal war.
Jheck
The Jheck are a mysterious and dangerous people, often described as D'ivers wolves. They are a type of shapeshifter, capable of transforming into multiple wolves. Their exact origins are debated, with some theories suggesting they are remnants of an ancient soletaken ritual in the land of Lether. The Jheck are a formidable and predatory force, known for their pack-like behavior and ferocity in battle.
Jhinan
Tribal people of northern Genabackis.
Kindaru
The Kindaru are a tribal people from the Lamatath Plain and are known to be the cousins of the Gandaru. Like their relatives, they are a reclusive and mysterious group, with a deep connection to their ancestral lands. While little is known about their specific customs, their shared ancestry with the Gandaru suggests a similar blend of human and ancient, animal-like traits, making them a unique and enigmatic presence in the Malazan world.
Korvalahrai
The Korvalahrai were a race of demon seafarers from the warren of Aral Gamelon. They spoke with the voices of children.
K'chain Che'Malle
The K'chain Che'Malle are an extinct mythical people, one of the Four Founding Races of the Malazan world. These reptilian beings once ruled vast territories and possessed advanced technology and devastating martial prowess before their civilization collapsed.
Moranth
A highly regimented people who were centred in the Cloud Forest of Genabackis. Their culture was largely closed off from outsiders, who found their ways something of a mystery.
Rhivi
The Rhivi are a nomadic people who dwell on the central plains of Genabackis, following the great herds in a seasonal cycle. They are a small, brown-skinned people who live in harmony with the land, building no permanent structures. Though not a warlike people, they were forced to ally with Caladan Brood against the Malazan Empire. Their culture is rich with unique customs, including a distinctive marriage ritual involving braided grass, and they revere a pantheon of nature spirits.
Saemdhi
The Saemdhi are an indigenous people of the northern tundra, believed to be closely related to the pre-Ritual Imass. For generations, they were held captive as part of a Jaghut experiment, imprisoned behind a vast wall of ice. With the melting of their prison, the Saemdhi have been released into a world that has long forgotten them, carrying with them the legacy of their ancient origins and their harrowing ordeal.
Seguleh
The Seguleh are a reclusive and enigmatic people from an island south of Morn, feared and respected for their martial prowess. Their society is a strict martial hierarchy, where rank is everything, displayed through markings on their masks. They are a warrior caste, and their entire culture revolves around combat and the pursuit of individual standing within their rigid system. The name 'Seguleh' is an old word for 'The Anvil,' hinting at a possible origin as a remnant of a forgotten empire's army.
Sinbarl
Tribal people of the Lamatath Plain.
Skathandi
The Skathandi are a tribal people from the Lamatath Plain, with a kingdom that was once a notable power in the region. Their history is marked by tragedy, including the crucifixion of their king and their involvement in the Pannion War. The Skathandi are a people with a deep and complex history, their legacy intertwined with the turbulent events of the Malazan world. Their culture and traditions are not widely known, adding to their enigmatic presence.
Teblor
The Teblor are a proud and isolated offshoot of the Thelomen Toblakai, a race of giants. Deliberately sheltered from the outside world, they have developed a seemingly primitive culture, having lost much of their ancestral knowledge. They are significantly larger and stronger than humans, with a fierce warrior tradition. The term 'Teblor' is often used to refer specifically to the tribes from which the famed warrior Karsa Orlong hails, and while related to the broader Toblakai, they are a distinct and formidable people in their own right.
Thelomen Tartheno Toblakai
The Thelomen Tartheno Toblakai are an intelligent humanoid race descended from the Thel Akai, said to be the blessed children of the Great Mother. Once a unified people, they scattered across the world long ago, with isolated tribes living unaware of their distant relatives on different continents. These beings are notably taller and more robust than humans, with distinctive broad, flat faces and pure-blooded individuals typically exceeding seven feet in height. They possess four lungs rather than two, and can interbreed with humans to produce viable offspring. Once great civilizations with cities and written knowledge, most contemporary populations had declined to tribal societies.
T'lan Imass
The T'lan Imass, also known as the Silent Host, are an undead Elder Race, one of the four Founding Races of the Malazan world. They were mortal Imass who underwent the Ritual of Tellann, which granted them immortality in exchange for their flesh, leaving them as sentient, mummified corpses. They are bound by an eternal war against the Jaghut. Their shamans are known as Bonecasters, and they possess the ability to travel great distances by turning to dust and riding the winds.
Tiste Andii
The Tiste Andii were a non-human Elder Race, Children of Darkness and first of Mother Dark's children, dwellers of the land before light and sometimes called the Souls of Starless Night; immortal and led by Archmage Anomander Rake, they once lived in Kurald Galain around their first city Kharkanas, worshipping Mother Dark until her withdrawal left them in turmoil and wandering under Rake's guidance.
Tiste Edur
The Tiste Edur were a non-human Elder Race known as the Children of Shadow, the third offspring of Mother Dark, originally from Kurald Emurlahn whose Elder magic warren still served them; when that warren was shattered, Edur led by Scabandari Bloodeye and Tiste Andii led by Silchas Ruin fled to the continent later called Lether, where after a brutal war with the K'Chain Che'Malle and betrayal of their Andii allies they settled in the northwest.
Tiste Liosan
The Tiste Liosan were a non-human elder race also known as the Children of Light, second of Mother Dark's children; not one of the four Founding Races, they reached the Malazan world only after a breach in the barriers of their Warren of Kurald Thyrllan, possibly via human use of Telas and Thyr and Imass use of the Tellann.
Trell
The Trell are a non-human pastoral nomadic warrior society in transition to sedentarianism. Once a powerful and widespread people, they are characterized by their massive build and cultural ties to the earth and stone.
