Anomander Rake
Son of Darkness and lord of Moon’s Spawn, the Tiste Andii leader whose ancient power, sense of duty, and grim honour shape much of Genabackis’s fate. He is also known as the Black-Winged Lord, a title that alludes to his ability to transform into a great, black-skinned dragon.
Apsal'ara
Also known as the Lady of Thieves, Apsal'ara was an ancient Imass Ascendant. She was killed by Anomander Rake with his sword Dragnipur and her soul was trapped within it for a long time. She is a separate entity from the mortal woman Apsalar, who took her name.
Beru
Beru, also known as the Lord of Storms, is an Ascendant and the son of the Elder God Mael. Often invoked by sailors through curses like "Beru fend!" or "by Beru's beard!", he holds dominion over tempests and the rages of the sea.
Burn
Burn is the sleeping goddess whose very dreams are the earth and all life upon it. Her slumber protects the world from her immense power, as her awakening would trigger catastrophic earthquakes and volcanic eruptions. She is slowly being poisoned by the influence of the Crippled God, and her fate is a central concern for many powers in the Malazan world.
Caladan Brood
A warlord allied with Anomander Rake, famed for his massive hammer, his resistance to the Malazan Empire, and his uneasy connection to elder powers.
Citravaghra
One of the Ascendants.
Cotillion
Cotillion (formerly Dancer), also known as the Rope, is the God of Assassins and patron of assassins. He ascended to godhood alongside Shadowthrone (Kellanved) and remains his constant companion and ally. Once a mortal killer, his subtlety and blade now move through the Warren of Shadow. Cotillion is frequently involved in mortal affairs, often manipulating events indirectly to achieve his goals. He is notably connected to Apsalar, whom he possessed and later mentored.
Crippled God
The Crippled God, also known as the Chained One, is a foreign god who was summoned to the Malazan world by mages. His fall to the world shattered his body and his divine power, and he was subsequently chained. His immense suffering and desire for freedom are a driving force behind many of the events in the series.
D'rek
D'rek, also known as the Worm of Autumn, is a god of decay, rot, and the changing of seasons. D'rek's gender is fluid, and the deity is strongly opposed to the influence of the Crippled God. Their followers believe that all things, in time, must crumble and return to the earth.
Dessembrae
The ascended form of Dassem Ultor, the legendary First Sword of the Malazan Empire. He is worshipped as the Lord of Tragedy and is the patron of soldiers, embodying their grief and loss in war. He is also the figure known as Traveller, who seeks to kill Hood, the Lord of Death.
Draconus
Draconus is an Elder God and the Suzerain of Night, an ancient and powerful being who embodies the power of Darkness. He was the consort of Mother Dark and the forger of the sword Dragnipur. He was eventually betrayed and killed by Anomander Rake, and his soul was imprisoned within the very sword he created.
Envy
Lady Envy is a powerful Ascendant sorceress, daughter of the Elder God Draconus. Renowned for her formidable magic, keen intellect, and magnetic charisma, she navigates the Malazan world with an inscrutable blend of vanity and cunning.
Eres'al
Eres'al is an ancient, possibly divine spirit of the Eres, known for her ability to traverse time and influence events to subtly "course-correct" history. Often acting as a motherly entity, she intervenes in the Malazan world when necessary, influencing various characters and plotlines.
Fanderay
Fanderay, known as "The She-Wolf of Winter," is an ancient wolf-god and the mate of Togg. Their reunion is a pivotal event, leading them to occupy the Thrones of the Beast Hold. As ascended beasts, they embody immense power and are deeply connected to the natural world and the House of War.

Oponn
The twin Jesters of Chance, Ascendants, and an Unaligned card in the Deck of Dragons. The twins were male and female. When he pushed bad luck followed, while when she pulled, good fortune smiled. Interfering in the lives of mortals was a game they conducted for their own entertainment and a way to disrupt the plans of other Gods. At the same time the tension of their dual nature was part of a never-ending personal game between themselves. The male twin was described as a young man with a thin, pale, expressionless face. He was clothed in loose, white and gold, foppish silk clothing. His slim female counterpart had blonde hair with a reddish tint and wore gaudy silks and a shimmering purple cape.

Shadowthrone
Also called Ammanas and once the mortal Emperor Kellanved, this Dal Honese mage co-founded the Malazan Empire with Dancer before vanishing into the Deadhouse and staging his own assassination. Ascending to seize the Throne of Shadow, he shares dominion of the Warren with Cotillion, commands its Hounds, and manipulates the High House Shadow and the Deck of Dragons through multilayered plots that reach across continents and elder warrens. His hunched, twitching presence masks encyclopedic knowledge of forgotten paths and an unrelenting drive to outmaneuver rival gods—from opposing the Crippled God to nudging mortal empires from behind the veil.

Whiskeyjack
A veteran commander of the Bridgeburners, he was a former protégé of Dassem Ultor. He commanded the Malazan 2nd Army and, at different times, the Malazan 3rd Army. He was also a sergeant in the 9th Squad of the Bridgeburners. His leadership, sacrifice, and mythic stature raise him close to the realm of Ascendants in memory and story.
