Baran
One of the ancient Hounds of Shadow, mate to Blind and often pacing at Shadowthrone's side as a heavy, brutal hunter-beast of the Warren of Shadow.
Baruk
Baruk was the High Alchemist and discreet leader of Darujhistan's nine-mage T'orrud Cabal, ruling from his Estate District manor and working to keep the city free of Malazan control; he ran a wide spy network of agents and familiars to safeguard its survival.
Bellurdan
T'lan Imass-blooded High Mage serving the Empress alongside Tayschrenn.
Blind
Scarred Hound of Shadow and favourite of Shadowthrone, its ruined eyes giving it its name yet leaving it no less cunning or deadly.
Calot
Cadre mage of the Malazan 2nd Army and Tattersail's lover during the Genabackis campaign.
Captain Stillis
Captain of the guard at Simtal Estate outside Darujhistan.
Cartheron Crust
A High Fist of the Malazan Empire in the Seven Cities, Cartheron Crust was a prominent figure in the Malazan Empire, serving as an admiral and a member of the Old Guard during Emperor Kellanved's era. He was known as the captain of the Ragstopper. During Laseen's rise to power, Cartheron and his brother, Urko Crust, were said to have "drowned," a euphemism for their defection from the empire. He also spent time in the Deadhouse.
Challice D'Arle
Daughter of Estraysian D'Arle, a noblewoman of Darujhistan.
Chert
Local bully in Darujhistan, often encountering misfortune around the Phoenix Inn.
Choss
A Sub-commander of the Malaz 3rd Army at the Siege of Y'Ghatan under Dassem Ultor, alongside Amaron and Whiskeyjack. He was also Nok’s protege. After Dassem was wounded, Choss received a field promotion to High Fist and became interim commander of the Third Army. He was a wiry, battle-scarred man with a bushy tangled head of curly hair, a gold and russet beard, and thick, expressive brows. Being Napan, he presumably had blue-hued skin.
Circle Breaker
Nervous city guard secretly serving as an agent of the Eel.
Coll
Dispossessed Daru noble and drunk, friend to Murillio and Kruppe.
Corporal Blues
Sixth Blade of the Crimson Guard, a veteran corporal among its elite.
Cowl
High Mage of the Crimson Guard, feared for his subtle sorcery.
Crokus Younghand
Young Darujhistan thief whose rooftop exploits draw him into events far beyond simple burglary.
Crone
Matriarchal leader of the Great Ravens inhabiting Moon's Spawn, commanding thousands of corvid kin with authority earned through immense age—over 120,000 years old, making her the eldest of her kind. Distinctive features marked her as formidable: glittering black eyes, enormous oily-feathered wings, and a notably broken beak that she referenced with dark humor. She maintained steadfast loyalty to Anomander Rake, serving as his favored aerial spy and critical intermediary, treating the ascendant as a near-equal rather than a master.
Dancer
Deadly assassin and closest adviser to Emperor Kellanved, slain during Laseen's rise to power.
Dassem Ultor
Legendary First Sword of the Empire, said to have fallen outside Y'Ghatan in Seven Cities.
D'Ebbin
A Malazan Fist, described as a short, round man with hairless bony eyebrows. He commanded the 4th Army's right flank under High Fist Anand during the Malazan civil war and the Battle of the Plains. He was later promoted to High Fist.
Derudan
Witch of Tennes and member of the T'orrud Cabal.
Doan
Largest of the Hounds of Shadow, first-born of Rood and its first mate Pallick, later slain by Anomander Rake and chained within the sword Dragnipur.
Dujek Onearm
High Fist commanding the Malazan armies in the Genabackis campaign, famed for his missing arm which had been severed just below the shoulder. He was considered one of the great commanders, inspiring a sense of security, stability and sanity in his followers. He despised tyranny.
Eel
Mysterious master-spy whose hidden agents influence events in Darujhistan.
Emperor Kellanved
Shadowy founder of the Malazan Empire, later overthrown and assassinated before the events in Darujhistan.

Kin Clip
One of the Great Ravens inhabiting Moon's Spawn. She served under Great Mother Crone, the raven matriarch, and worked alongside Anomander Rake. Her fellow great raven, Hurtle, twice referred to her as Kin Clip and once called her simply Clip, suggesting "Kin" may have functioned as a kinship indicator rather than part of her formal name.

Lorn
Adjunct to Empress Laseen, the cold and implacable representative of imperial will. Young and with long auburn hair, she was an accomplished rider capable of mastering a warhorse stallion bred of the finest lines of Seven Cities stock.
She carried an Otataral longsword which negated sorcery. Possibly the sword itself or her long association with it gave her the ability to know the patterns of sorcery and the pattern of the minds that used it.

Pormqual
High Fist of the Seven Cities continent, based in Aren. He was described as tall, wiry, pale, and slope-shouldered with a thin, oiled face, wearing "clothes that would beggar a king." Despite owning stables of fine horses, he was a poor rider. Pormqual was despised by the Red Blades. During the Whirlwind rebellion, Pormqual readied to abandon Aren, evacuating his household and prized breeding stallions. He was betrayed by Mallick Rel and beheaded by Korbolo Dom.
